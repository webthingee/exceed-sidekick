# Anatomy of a Strike

The full structure of a Strike, step by step. When a player initiates a Strike (as their action or from a Boost), they are the **Attacker** and their opponent is the **Defender**.

## Set attacks

1. The Attacker sets attack card(s) face-down. Characters with “when striking” effects do those **before** setting cards.
2. The Defender sets the same way.
3. Reveal both attacks.

**Ways to set an attack:**

| Type | How | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Regular** | One card from hand, face-down | — |
| **EX** | Two cards with the **same name** from hand | +1 Power, Speed, Armor, and Guard |
| **Wild Swing** | Top card of your deck, face-down (don’t look) | Required if you have no cards in hand |

**Critical:** When you set any attack, you may spend **1 Gauge**. If you do, Critical effects on your attack and character ability apply this Strike. You may pay even with no Critical text (as a bluff).

## Reveal

- **EX** — Discard the extra copy.
- **Ultra** — Pay its Gauge cost now, or the attack is **Invalid**. EX Ultra pays once. A Wild Swing Ultra may be chosen Invalid.
- **Invalid** — Discard it and Wild Swing; repeat until you reveal a valid attack. These replacements are **not** a new “set.”

## Priority

Higher **Speed** goes first (**Active**). On a tie, the **Attacker** goes first. The other player is **Reactive**.

## Active player attack

1. **Before** effects  
2. Check **range** to the opponent (from the attack’s origin — usually your character)  
3. If in range: **Hit** effects, then deal damage  
4. **After** effects  

### Damage, Armor, Guard, Stun

- Damage = your **Power** (life loss).
- Opponent’s **Armor** reduces damage taken during that attack.
- If damage dealt over the attack is **greater than** their **Guard**, they are **Stunned** and skip their attack.
- Missing Armor/Guard prints as **0**. Armor only absorbs damage during that attack; later damage on the same turn isn’t blocked by the same Armor again.

## Reactive player attack

If not Stunned, the Reactive player resolves their attack the same way. If Stunned, skip it.

## Cleanup

Attacker does steps 1–4 first, then Defender; then step 5:

1. Resolve **Cleanup** triggers on your attack / Continuous Boosts.  
2. Discard Continuous Boosts in your boost area (unless **sustained**).  
3. If you **hit** (even without stunning), put your attack in **Gauge**; otherwise discard it.  
4. Resolve **End of the Strike** effects (even if Stunned).  
5. Next turn is normally the **Defender’s**. If someone gained **Advantage**, they take the next turn instead. If both did, the one who gained it **most recently** goes.

## Triggers (quick reference)

| Trigger | When |
|---|---|
| **Now** | Immediately (often the instant half of a Continuous Boost) |
| **Before** | Start of your attack, before range check |
| **Hit** | Only if you hit |
| **After** | End of your attack (hit or miss) |
| **Cleanup** | End of Strike (often to sustain a Continuous Boost) |

You must activate each matching trigger; if several share a timing, you choose the order.

## Movement keywords (for reference)

Effects use these movement types (card/boost movement usually costs no Force):

- **Close** — toward opponent, as far as possible, **without** switching sides  
- **Advance** — toward opponent, may switch sides and continue  
- **Retreat** — away from opponent  
- **Move** — either direction, may switch sides  
- **Push / Pull** — move the **opponent** (away / toward; Pull can pass you)

You can never share a space; moving into the opponent’s space moves past them instead. Move as far as possible if you can’t go the full distance.
