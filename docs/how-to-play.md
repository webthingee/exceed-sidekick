# How to Play: Step-by-Step

A walk-through of the basic loop of a standard two-player game of Exceed.

Randomly decide who goes first — rock-paper-scissors, a die roll, whatever works!

## Setup

1. **Build your deck.** Shuffle your **30-card** deck:
   - **Normals** — 2 copies each of Grasp, Cross, Assault, Dive, Spike, Sweep, Focus, and Block (same for every character).
   - **Character cards** — 2 copies of each of your fighter’s 5 Special Attacks and 2 Ultra Attacks.
2. **Set the arena.** Place the **9-space** board. Put the fighters on spaces **3** and **7**, Normal side up. Each player starts at **30 life** (life can never go above 30).
3. **Draw opening hands.** Draw **5** cards each. The player going second draws **1 extra** card. Maximum hand size is **7** — if you ever have more after your end-of-turn draw, discard down to 7.
4. **Mulligan.** Discard any number of cards from your hand, then draw that many. Shuffle the discarded cards back into your deck. Don’t show your opponent what you discarded.
5. **Begin.** The first player takes the first turn.

## The turn loop

On your turn, choose **one** of these:

| Action | What it does |
|---|---|
| **Prepare** | Draw an extra card (plus your end-of-turn draw → 2 cards this turn). |
| **Change Cards** | Spend Force up front; draw 1 card per Force spent. |
| **Move** | Spend Force to Advance or Retreat (1 Force per space; +1 Force total if you Advance past the opponent). |
| **Boost** | Pay a card’s Force cost and resolve its Boost. |
| **Exceed** | Pay the Gauge cost on your character card; flip to Exceed Mode. |
| **Reshuffle** | Shuffle discard into a new deck (**once per game**, same as the automatic reshuffle). |
| **Strike** | Both players set attacks face-down and resolve combat. |

Some card effects grant extra actions (“As an action…”).

**End of turn:** If you did **not** Strike, draw **1** card. (A Strike from a Boost still counts as Striking — no draw.)

## Force and Gauge

- **Force** pays for Move, Change Cards, and Boosts. Generate it only as you spend it by discarding Normals/Specials (**1 Force** each) or Ultras (**1 or 2 Force**, your choice) from hand or Gauge.
- **Gauge** is built by **hitting** with attacks (those cards go to your Gauge Area in Cleanup). Spend it to play Ultras, Exceed, activate **Criticals**, or as Force.

## Keep playing

Players alternate turns — repositioning, boosting, and striking — until someone wins:

- Reduce the opponent to **0 life**, **or**
- They need to draw / Wild Swing with an empty deck **after** they’ve already used their one reshuffle.

While there is much more detail (especially during Strikes), this is the core loop of Exceed. For combat step-by-step, see **[Anatomy of a Strike](./anatomy-of-a-strike.md)**. For solo play against the AI, see **[Solo Rules](./solo-rules.md)**.
